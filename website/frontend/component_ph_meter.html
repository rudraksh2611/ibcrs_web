pH Meter Detection

Acidity & Alkalinity Measurement Equipment

Introduction

A pH meter is a laboratory instrument used to measure the acidity or alkalinity of a solution. It determines the pH value, which indicates how acidic or basic a liquid is on a scale from 0 to 14. pH meters are widely used in laboratories, agriculture, water quality testing, food processing, and research applications.

Advantages

  • Provides accurate and precise pH measurements.
  • Faster and more reliable than pH paper methods.
  • Easy to operate and suitable for various applications.
  • Can measure a wide range of solutions.
  • Portable models available for field testing.

How It Works

The pH meter consists of a special glass electrode and a reference electrode.

When the electrode is dipped into a solution, it detects hydrogen ion concentration.

The electrode generates a small electrical signal based on the acidity or alkalinity of the solution.

The meter converts this electrical signal into a pH value.

The pH value is then displayed on the digital screen for easy reading.
